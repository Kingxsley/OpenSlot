This Privacy Policy explains how [Operator legal name] (“Enjeeoh”, “we”, “us”) collects, uses, shares and protects personal data when you use the Enjeeoh scheduling platform and websites (the “Service”). We are committed to handling personal data lawfully, fairly and transparently.

Two roles to be clear about. For account holders (organisations and their members) we are the data controller. For people who book appointments through an organisation’s page, that organisation is the controller and we act as its data processor — see our Data Processing Addendum.

1. Data we collect

Organisation & account data

  • Organisation details: organisation name, country, website, registration/charity identifier, mission statement, and the contact you provide at sign-up.
  • Member accounts: name, email address, role, working hours/availability, a public booking slug, and a securely hashed password. If you enable two-factor authentication we store a TOTP secret and one-time recovery codes (hashed).

Booking data (collected for organisations)

  • The name, email address, chosen time, and any notes provided by a person booking an appointment through an organisation’s page, plus the resulting calendar invitation and video-meeting link.

Technical & security data

  • Activity & audit logs: actions taken in the platform (e.g. sign-ins, approvals, changes), with timestamps, the acting account, and the originating IP address. We use these to operate the Service securely, investigate abuse, and meet our obligations.
  • Session cookies: a small number of strictly necessary cookies described in our Cookie Policy. We do not use advertising or third-party tracking cookies.

2. How we use personal data

  • To create and operate accounts, authenticate users, and provide the booking and scheduling features.
  • To send transactional emails (account set-up, invitations, password resets and reminders, booking confirmations, and verification decisions).
  • To verify the eligibility of organisations applying to use the platform.
  • To keep the Service secure — detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, brute-force attempts and unauthorised access (for example, temporary account lockout after repeated failed sign-ins).
  • To respond to support requests and enforce our Terms and Acceptable Use Policy.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell personal data, and we do not use it for behavioural advertising.

4. When we share data

We share personal data only as needed to run the Service:

  • Within an organisation: a member’s bookings are visible to that organisation’s admins and managers.
  • With the service providers (sub-processors) listed below, strictly to deliver the Service.
  • Where required by law, to comply with a valid legal request, or to protect the rights, safety and property of users or the public.
  • On a business transfer, personal data may be transferred as part of a merger, acquisition or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

6. International transfers

Our providers may process data outside your country, including outside the UK/EEA. Where they do, transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision.

7. Data retention

  • Account and organisation data is retained for as long as the account is active. When an organisation is deleted, its members, bookings and related data are removed.
  • Booking data is retained on behalf of the organisation according to its instructions.
  • Security and audit logs are retained for a limited period appropriate to their purpose and then rotated.
  • We may retain limited information for longer where required to comply with legal obligations or resolve disputes.

8. How we protect data

  • Passwords are stored only as salted bcrypt hashes — never in plain text.
  • Optional two-factor authentication (TOTP) and a configurable password policy, including expiry and reuse-prevention reminders.
  • Automatic temporary lockout after repeated failed sign-in attempts.
  • Session tokens are held in HttpOnly cookies (not readable by page scripts), with CSRF protection on state-changing requests and per-IP rate limiting on authentication endpoints.
  • Security response headers (including a Content-Security-Policy) and transport encryption (HTTPS) in production.
  • Tenant isolation so each organisation’s data is segregated.

No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect personal data using measures appropriate to the risk.

9. Your rights

Depending on your location you may have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict or object to processing of your personal data, and to data portability. You can exercise many of these directly in your dashboard, or contact us. If your data was provided to an organisation when booking, please contact that organisation (the controller); we will assist them as their processor.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

10. Children

The Service is intended for organisations and their staff/volunteers, not for children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us data, contact us and we will delete it.
