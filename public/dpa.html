Data Processing Addendum

Last updated: [date]

This Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) forms part of the Terms of Service between [Operator legal name] (“Enjeeoh”, “Processor”) and the verified organisation using the Service (“Organisation”, “Controller”). It applies where Enjeeoh processes personal data of third parties (for example, people who book appointments) on the Organisation’s behalf.

Roles: the Organisation is the data controller of the booking data it collects from the public; Enjeeoh is the data processor acting on the Organisation’s documented instructions. For the Organisation’s own account data, Enjeeoh is the controller under its Privacy Policy.

1. Subject matter & instructions

Enjeeoh processes personal data only to provide the Service and on the Organisation’s documented instructions (including via the Service’s settings), unless required by law. If we believe an instruction breaches applicable law, we will inform the Organisation.

2. Details of processing

ItemDetail
Subject matterProvision of scheduling/booking functionality
DurationFor the term of the Organisation’s use of the Service
Nature & purposeReceiving and storing bookings; sending confirmations; creating meeting links
Types of dataNames, email addresses, appointment times, booking notes, meeting links
Categories of data subjectPeople who book appointments with the Organisation

3. Confidentiality

Enjeeoh ensures that persons authorised to process the personal data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.

4. Security

Enjeeoh implements appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including those described in our Privacy Policy (password hashing, optional MFA, account lockout, HttpOnly session cookies, CSRF protection, rate limiting, security headers, transport encryption, and tenant isolation).

5. Sub-processors

The Organisation authorises Enjeeoh to engage the sub-processors listed in our Privacy Policy (hosting, database, email delivery, and video meetings). Enjeeoh imposes data-protection obligations on each sub-processor and remains responsible for their performance. We will give notice of intended changes to sub-processors so the Organisation can object on reasonable grounds.

6. Assistance to the Controller

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Enjeeoh will provide reasonable assistance to help the Organisation respond to data-subject requests and meet its obligations regarding security, breach notification, and impact assessments.

7. Data-subject requests

If Enjeeoh receives a request from a data subject relating to booking data, we will, where lawful, direct it to the Organisation and assist the Organisation in responding.

8. Personal data breaches

Enjeeoh will notify the Organisation without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Organisation’s data, with information reasonably available to assist the Organisation’s own notification duties.

9. International transfers

Where sub-processors process data outside the Organisation’s jurisdiction, appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision) apply.

10. Return & deletion

On termination, or on the Organisation’s instruction, Enjeeoh will delete the Organisation’s personal data (including by deleting the organisation account and its associated bookings), except where retention is required by law.

11. Audit

Enjeeoh will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and will allow for and contribute to reasonable audits, subject to confidentiality and reasonable notice.

12. Liability & order of precedence

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations in the Terms of Service. If there is a conflict between this DPA and the Terms regarding data processing, this DPA prevails.
